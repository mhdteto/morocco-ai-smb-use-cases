# ROI Estimation Guide

## Overview

AI ROI for SMBs should be estimated carefully.

The goal is not to promise exact financial results.

The goal is to estimate whether a use case is worth testing.

---

## Common value drivers

AI and automation can create value through:

- time saved;
- faster response time;
- better lead qualification;
- fewer manual errors;
- improved content production;
- better customer support;
- more consistent follow-up;
- cleaner CRM data;
- faster reporting.

---

## Simple ROI proxy

Use this simple formula:

```txt
Monthly value = hours saved per month x estimated hourly value

ROI proxy = monthly value - monthly AI/tool/process cost
```

---

## Example

```txt
Task: Lead qualification
Time saved per lead: 10 minutes
Leads per month: 120

Total minutes saved: 1,200
Total hours saved: 20

Estimated hourly value: 25
Monthly value: 500

Monthly tool/process cost: 100

ROI proxy: 400 per month
```

---

## Productivity value by task

| Use case | Example time saved |
|---|---:|
| Lead qualification | 5–15 minutes per lead |
| Follow-up email drafting | 5–10 minutes per email |
| Meeting summary | 10–20 minutes per meeting |
| Review summarization | 1–3 hours per month |
| Content brief generation | 30–90 minutes per brief |
| CRM note structuring | 5–15 minutes per opportunity |

These are directional examples, not guaranteed results.

---

## What not to do

Do not calculate ROI based only on optimistic assumptions.

Avoid:

- assuming 100% adoption;
- ignoring review time;
- ignoring setup time;
- ignoring data cleanup;
- assuming every saved minute becomes productive revenue;
- claiming revenue growth without measurement.
