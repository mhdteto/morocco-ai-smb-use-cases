# SMB AI Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate whether a small or medium business is ready to start with AI.

## 1. Business clarity

- [ ] The business has clear services or products.
- [ ] The target customers are known.
- [ ] The main business problems are clear.
- [ ] The team knows which tasks are repetitive.
- [ ] There are clear operational bottlenecks.
- [ ] The business has at least one measurable objective.

## 2. Data readiness

- [ ] Customer inquiries are stored somewhere.
- [ ] Sales leads are tracked.
- [ ] Basic customer data is available.
- [ ] Product or service information is documented.
- [ ] FAQs or common questions are known.
- [ ] Important business documents are accessible.
- [ ] Data is not only stored in scattered conversations.

## 3. Process readiness

- [ ] The business has repeatable workflows.
- [ ] Leads follow a known process.
- [ ] Customer requests are handled consistently.
- [ ] Team members know who does what.
- [ ] There is a basic way to track tasks.
- [ ] There is a clear owner for AI or automation projects.

## 4. Tool readiness

- [ ] The business uses email professionally.
- [ ] The business uses a spreadsheet, CRM or management tool.
- [ ] The business has a website or landing page.
- [ ] The business has public profiles such as LinkedIn or Google Business Profile.
- [ ] The business can access basic analytics or sales data.
- [ ] The business can test simple tools without disrupting operations.

## 5. Adoption readiness

- [ ] The team is open to testing AI.
- [ ] The owner or manager supports the pilot.
- [ ] There is time to review outputs.
- [ ] There is a process for human approval.
- [ ] The business is willing to improve workflows, not just add tools.
- [ ] The business can start small.

## Score interpretation

| Score | Readiness level |
|---:|---|
| 0–8 | Not ready yet |
| 9–16 | Basic readiness |
| 17–24 | Good readiness |
| 25–30 | Strong readiness |

## Recommendation

If readiness is low, start with documentation and process clarity before automation.

If readiness is medium, start with simple use cases such as:

- FAQ assistant;
- lead qualification;
- content brief generation;
- review summarization;
- sales follow-up drafts.

If readiness is high, test a workflow that connects multiple tools, such as:

- form to CRM;
- email classifier;
- meeting summary to CRM;
- quote generation;
- lead scoring and routing.
