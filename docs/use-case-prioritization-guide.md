# AI Use Case Prioritization Guide

## Overview

Not every AI use case is worth doing first.

A good first use case should be:

- valuable;
- simple enough to test;
- connected to a real workflow;
- measurable;
- low risk;
- easy for the team to adopt.

This guide helps prioritize AI opportunities for SMBs.

---

## Prioritization dimensions

Score each use case from 1 to 5.

### 1. Business value

How much value could this create?

Consider:

- time saved;
- revenue impact;
- lead quality;
- customer experience;
- error reduction;
- operational speed.

### 2. Complexity

How hard is it to implement?

Consider:

- number of tools involved;
- data quality;
- integrations;
- process clarity;
- human review;
- technical setup.

### 3. Data readiness

Is the required information available?

Consider:

- documents;
- FAQs;
- CRM data;
- email history;
- product catalog;
- pricing rules;
- customer questions.

### 4. Adoption readiness

Will the team use it?

Consider:

- workflow fit;
- ease of use;
- training required;
- manager support;
- time to review output.

## Recommended first pilots

For most SMBs, good first pilots include:

1. Lead qualification.
2. FAQ assistant.
3. Sales follow-up draft generator.
4. Customer review summarizer.
5. Content brief generator.
6. Meeting summary to CRM.
7. Quote request classifier.

Avoid starting with complex autonomous agents or fully automated customer-facing decisions.
